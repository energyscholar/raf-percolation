\begin{abstract}
Reflexively autocatalytic, food-generated (RAF) sets emerge with high probability
above a sharp catalysis probability threshold in random polymer networks.
This threshold behavior has been extensively characterized within the
origin-of-life literature using the framework of autocatalytic set theory.
Here we show that RAF emergence maps formally onto a site percolation
problem on the catalysis graph, placing the RAF threshold within the
well-studied universality class of percolation transitions.
This identification connects two mature literatures---autocatalytic set
theory and percolation theory---that have developed independently
despite describing the same mathematical phenomenon.
We derive the correspondence, verify it numerically, and discuss
implications for early warning signals of autocatalytic emergence
in prebiotic chemistry.
\end{abstract}
